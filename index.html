The Two-Tiered System: District vs. School

Understanding EdTech procurement requires knowing where decisions are made. The vast majority of significant financial decisions, especially for infrastructure, core software (SIS, LMS), and large hardware rollouts, happen at the district level. This centralizes purchasing power and ensures standardization.

School-level personnel, led by the Principal, control smaller, supplemental budgets. Their primary role is implementation, but their feedback and pilot programs heavily influence future district-wide adoptions. They are the "boots on the ground" whose buy-in is critical for success.

While schools have influence, the district holds the purse strings for major investments.

District HQ: The Central Command

Key District Role Profiles

CTO / CIO

Function: Sets tech vision, manages infrastructure, ensures security & interoperability.

Influence: High. Final technical decision-maker and budget manager for IT.

Pain Points: Legacy systems, data security threats, integration nightmares, tight budgets, proving ROI.

Director of Curriculum & Instruction

Function: Evaluates tools for pedagogical value, ensures alignment with standards, manages professional development.

Influence: High. Final academic decision-maker. Can veto tech that doesn't meet learning goals.

Pain Points: Teacher adoption, finding tools that are *actually* effective (not just "fun"), data privacy, lack of PD time.

Director of IT

Function: Manages day-to-day operations, help desk, device deployment, and network stability.

Influence: Medium-High. Key recommender to CTO. Manages implementation.

Pain Points: "Ticket-mill" fatigue, device breakage, network bandwidth, managing 1,000s of student accounts.

The Schoolhouse: Implementation & Influence

School-level staff are the end-users and on-site managers of technology. While they rarely make large purchases, their influence is critical. They pilot programs, discover new supplemental tools, and are the source of feedback that bubbles up to the district.

Key School Role Profiles

Principal

Function: Building-level manager, sets school culture, manages site budget, evaluates teachers.

Influence: Medium. Controls site-based budget for supplemental software. Key voice in district pilots.

Pain Points: Teacher burnout, student behavior, parent communication, pressure to raise test scores.

IT Specialist / Tech Coordinator

Function: First line of defense for all tech issues. "Fixes the Wi-Fi," resets passwords, manages carts.

Influence: Medium. Strong recommender to district IT. Knows what *actually* works vs. what breaks.

Pain Points: Overwhelmed with requests, lack of time for proactive training, users who don't follow instructions.

Lead Teacher / Dept. Head

Function: Mentors other teachers, pilots new software, advocates for subject-specific tools.

Influence: Low-Medium. Highly trusted by peers and curriculum directors. A "teacher champion" is essential for adoption.

Pain Points: Tools that are hard to use, "one more login" fatigue, lack of student engagement, tools that don't help with differentiation.
